Step 3. Solve the Cube!


It is very easy to make a mistake during this step, so I recommend reading the whole thing, including the common mistakes, before attempting it.
Turn the cube over so that the unsolved pieces are all in the bottom layer.
You may have 2, 3, or 4 unsolved pieces.



Repeatedly do the 4-move sequence until the front/right piece is solved (has yellow on the bottom).
Then turn the bottom layer (not the whole cube) to bring an unsolved piece to the front/right. Repeat until the whole cube is solved.

Common Mistakes

  • Turning the whole cube to get the next piece to the bottom/right. Make sure you turn only the bottom layer.
  • Not finishing the 4-moves because the piece is solved after 3 moves. Make sure you always finish the 4-moves.
Note: If you only have 1 piece unsolved while the rest of the cube is solved, or if you followed step 7 correctly but it does not work, then your cube is unsolvable, and needs to be taken apart and reassembled.
