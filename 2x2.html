Solving a 2x2


For a video tutorial click here

This is an easy 2x2 Rubik's Cube tutorial, where you don't need to learn move notation or long algorithms.
With some practice, you should easily be able to solve the cube in under 1 minute. If you want to get even faster, you should learn How to do Finger Tricks and Advanced 2x2 Methods.

Step 1. White Layer



Before solving anything, do the following 4-move sequence repeatedly until you have it memorized. It will be very important in Step 1 and Step 3!

4-move sequence



Find any white piece, and hold the cube so that white faces the bottom. This solves the first piece of the white layer.

Now we'll use the 4-moves to solve the first layer.
There are three remaining locations for the white piece to go:

With the solved white piece always on the bottom, find another white piece in the top layer. Examples:

If the new piece and solved piece share a color (besides white), then the new piece belongs on that side of the solved piece. Examples:

Here both the new piece and the solved piece have red in common, so the new piece will belong to the right of the solved piece so that both the red colours match.

Here both the new puece and the solved piece have blue in common, so the new piece will belong to the left of the solved piece so that both the blue colours match.


If the new piece and solved piece do not share a color (besides white), then the new piece belongs across from the solved piece. Example:
Here the two pieces don't have any colour in common (ie they are opposite colours to each other), so the new piece will belong on the opposite side of the layer as the solved piece.

Once the new piece is on the front/right, repeat the 4-moves until this piece is solved.

The pictures show the results after the 4-move sequence has been applied to the cube.
Repeat until all of the first layer pieces are solved. If find a white piece incorrectly stuck in the bottom layer, you can bring it into the top layer by holding it on the front/right and doing the 4-moves. The tutorial video above shows an example of this.
